Superscripts and Subscripts

It is easy to make superscripts and subscripts appear in your webpage. All you need to do is use the sup and sub tags.

Superscripts

Superscripts are small pieces of texts put above other texts. If you've seen exponents in math, those are considered superscripts.

To use superscripts in your web page, enclose a piece of text with the sup tag. Here's an example:

c2 = a2 + b2

This is the Pythagorean theorem and it uses exponents. For us to replicate the 'exponent' effect, we can use the superscript tags.

Subscripts

Subscripts are small pieces of texts located below other text. An example would be the numbers you see in chemical formulas.

Notice how when you write H2O the number 2 is below the other letters? That's a subscript.

To write a subscript, just use the sub tag to wrap around a piece of text. Here's an example.

H2 + O2 = H2O

This is the chemical equation for water or H2O and how it is created when Hydrogen and Oxygen molecules interact.
